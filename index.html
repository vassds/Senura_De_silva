About

I’m Senura De Silva, a 21-year-old cybersecurity enthusiast and second-year student at the National Institute of Business Management (NIBM), specializing in Network Engineering and Ethical Hacking. My journey into cybersecurity began with a deep curiosity about how systems communicate — and how they can be exploited or defended. That curiosity has evolved into a full-blown mission to understand the digital battlefield inside and out. I’m passionate about exploring both offensive and defensive security — from penetration testing and network hardening to incident response and threat analysis. I believe that to build secure systems, one must first learn how to break them. That’s the philosophy that drives my learning every day. Beyond academics, I’m an active CTF (Capture The Flag) player, always pushing myself to discover new vulnerabilities, exploit logic flaws, and learn creative ways to secure systems. Each challenge strengthens my analytical thinking, scripting skills, and ability to approach problems from an attacker’s mindset. When I’m not buried in code or network packets, I love designing clean and minimal UIs — merging technology, security, and creativity. I aim to bridge technical depth with visual simplicity, making security both functional and beautiful. My goal is to become a well-rounded cybersecurity engineer, capable of handling everything from red teaming and network defense to system hardening and threat mitigation. I see cybersecurity not just as a career, but as a lifestyle — a continuous journey of learning, experimenting, and evolving.
